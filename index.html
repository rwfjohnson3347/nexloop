Capabilities

Built for operational teams.

Three core problems that drive unplanned downtime and maintenance blind spots — addressed with practical, deployable tooling.

Equipment Monitoring

Continuous sensing across critical assets — motors, pumps, compressors, and conveyors — with configurable alert thresholds and anomaly detection tuned to each machine's operating profile.

Predictive Diagnostics

Signal analysis that separates normal operational variance from developing faults. Maintenance teams get lead time and fault context — not just an alarm that something is wrong.

Maintenance Visibility

Clear reporting on asset condition, service history, and upcoming intervention needs — structured so maintenance planners and operations supervisors can both act on it without translation.

How We Work

Operational quickly. No year-long integration.

Nexloop is designed for fast, low-disruption deployment. We work alongside your maintenance team — not around them.

01

Site Assessment

We identify the highest-risk assets and failure modes with your team before installing anything. The deployment scope is defined by your actual exposure, not a default template.

02

Sensor Deployment

Wireless sensors fitted to critical equipment — no production interruption, no hardwiring, no modifications to existing control systems or PLCs.

03

Baseline and Calibration

We establish normal operating signatures for each asset across load conditions before tuning detection thresholds to your specific environment and tolerance for false positives.

04

Ongoing Monitoring

Your team receives alerts with operational context — what's degrading, at what rate, and what intervention is indicated — so they can plan, not react.

Where We Work

Industrial environments that can't afford surprises.

Nexloop is suited to facilities where unplanned equipment failure has direct production, safety, or cost consequences — and where maintenance teams are managing complex asset populations with limited visibility.

  • Manufacturing and assembly lines
  • Food and beverage processing
  • Water and wastewater treatment
  • Mining and bulk material handling
  • Industrial HVAC and utilities
  • Packaging and logistics equipment

Problem

Maintenance teams respond to failures after they happen — with no warning and no time to plan parts, labour, or production impact.

What changes

Equipment degradation surfaces early. Teams schedule intervention during planned downtime windows rather than scrambling on failure.

Problem

Preventive maintenance schedules are based on calendar intervals, not actual machine condition — generating unnecessary work and masking real risk.

What changes

Maintenance is driven by equipment state rather than elapsed time. High-risk assets get more attention; healthy ones get less.
